Profile

Researcher at the intersection of code, data, and applied economics.

I am pursuing a B.S. in Computer Science and a B.S. in Econometrics and Quantitative Economics at the University of South Florida's Genshaft Honors College, with a minor in Geographic Information Systems and a 4.0 GPA.

My work spans graph machine learning, spatial analysis, synthetic data generation, economic modeling, and financial technology. I have built software in Python, C++, R, C, C#, VBA, ReactJS, and tools including PyTorch/PyG, Pandas, Git, ArcGIS Pro, Terra, and ggplot2.

Research/Publications

Graph learning, spatial infrastructure, and applied data science.

CSAIL

Connected Social Artificial Intelligence Lab

Leading undergraduate work on Florida Blue Digital Twins, using LLMs and graph methods for synthetic generation of electronic health records. Developed EHR-Walk, a random walk algorithm for synthetic data on heterogeneous EHR graphs.

Spatial Lab

EV charging infrastructure disparity

Working with Dr. Ran Tao's Spatial and Transportation Lab on machine learning and spatial analysis for public EV charging infrastructure growth and accessibility across the United States.

REU

ReCYCLE Net

NSF REU research at James Madison University on cycle-aware graph neural networks for community detection, under Dr. Behnaz Moradi-Jamei.

Thesis

Nuclear power plants and local education

Honors thesis applying reduced-form economic techniques to study whether increased taxation of nuclear power plants produces local educational benefits across more than 20 states.
